1.1 Aim of the institution, importance of the RDM

The goal definition focuses on the importance of the RD policy for the respective institution. It should be related to the institution’s understanding of research. If other related policies, guidelines, and guidelines, etc. already exist, it is important to place the FD policy in this context.

1.1.1 What is the goal of establishing the research data policy?

The goal of establishing a research data policy in the context of the B3D Cluster is to ensure that the research data generated by the cluster’s activities is collected, managed, and shared in a way that supports the cluster’s research goals and objectives. The policy should outline the principles, guidelines, and procedures that researchers within the cluster should follow when working with research data.

Having a clear and consistent research data policy can help to promote good research practices and encourage collaboration and sharing among researchers within the cluster. It can also help to ensure that the cluster’s research data is properly managed and preserved, so that it can be accessed and used by other researchers in the future.

In addition, a research data policy can help to ensure that the cluster complies with legal and ethical standards, such as those related to data protection and privacy. It can also help to protect the cluster’s reputation and credibility by ensuring that its research data is trustworthy and reliable.

1.1.2 What fundamental importance does the institution attach to Research Data Management for science and society?

Research Data Management is considered to be of fundamental importance for both science and society. This is because it ensures that research data is properly collected, organized, and preserved, which makes it easier for other researchers to access and use the data. This, in turn, helps to advance scientific knowledge and enables researchers to build upon the work of others. Additionally, research data management also helps to ensure that research is conducted in a responsible and ethical manner, which is important for maintaining the public’s trust in science.

1.1.3 What significance does RDM have for the university or college and research?

Research Data Management is considered to be of significant importance for universities and colleges that conduct research. This is because it ensures that research data is properly collected, organized, and preserved, which makes it easier for researchers at the institution to access and use the data. This, in turn, helps to advance scientific knowledge and enables researchers to build upon the work of others. Additionally, research data management also helps to ensure that research is conducted in a responsible and ethical manner, which is important for maintaining the integrity of the research conducted at the institution.

1.2 Standards and principles relating to RDM

At this point, the cornerstones for a successful RDM are defined. A reference can be made to the rules of good scientific practice and/or to the peculiarities of the subject cultures.

1.2.1 According to which standards should the RDM be carried out?

Research Data Management should be carried out according to the principles of good scientific practice, as well as any specific guidelines or regulations that may be relevant to the subject area or institution involved. This may include standards for data collection, organization, preservation, and sharing, as well as ethical considerations related to the use of research data. Additionally, it may be important to consider the unique requirements and challenges of the subject area, such as the large volumes of data generated by radio astronomy, and develop appropriate strategies for managing and analyzing this data.

1.2.2 Should reference be made to the rules of good scientific practice?

Yes, it is advisable to reference the rules of good scientific practice when defining the cornerstones for successful research data management. The principles of good scientific practice provide a set of guidelines that help to ensure the integrity, transparency, and reproducibility of scientific research. These principles include principles such as honesty, objectivity, fairness, transparency, and accountability, which are essential for promoting trust in scientific research and ensuring that research is conducted in a responsible and ethical manner. By referencing the rules of good scientific practice, the proposed initiative can demonstrate its commitment to upholding these principles and ensuring that research data is managed in a way that is consistent with these principles. This can help to build trust and confidence in the research being conducted, and can help to promote the long-term success and sustainability of the initiative.

1.2.3 Are there institutional recommendations for good scientific practice?

There are institutional recommendations for good scientific practice in the context of this initiative. Good scientific practice refers to a set of principles and guidelines that researchers should follow in order to conduct high-quality, ethical research. These guidelines can vary depending on the institution and field of research, but generally include principles such as honesty, transparency, and reproducibility. It is not specified in the text whether the participating institutions have specific recommendations for good scientific practice, or whether they will be following a specific set of guidelines in their research data management.

1.2.4 What is the relationship between RDM and good scientific practice?

The relationship between research data management and good scientific practice is that good scientific practice involves conducting research in a transparent and reproducible manner, and part of achieving this transparency and reproducibility involves effective management of research data. This includes things like keeping detailed and accurate records of data collection and analysis, maintaining the integrity and security of the data, and making the data available for verification and reuse by others. By following these principles, researchers can ensure that their work is conducted in a reliable and trustworthy manner, and that their findings can be independently verified and built upon by other researchers.

1.2.5 Should the special features of the subject cultures be addressed?

It is generally a good idea to take into account the special features of subject cultures when defining the cornerstones for successful research data management. This can help ensure that the research data management practices are tailored to the specific needs and requirements of the research field, and can lead to more effective and efficient management of research data. Additionally, addressing the peculiarities of the subject cultures can help to ensure that the research data management practices are in line with the norms and standards of the research community, which can improve the credibility and trustworthiness of the research findings.

It is recommended to define central terms such as research data and research data management in the text. This can help ensure that all readers have a clear understanding of the concepts being discussed, and can help avoid confusion or misunderstandings.
