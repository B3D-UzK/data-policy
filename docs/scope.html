2  Scope

2.1 Range

A target group definition ensures that the addressees are clearly named and feel ad- dressed. As a rule, the target group includes all persons working at the institution (re- searchers, visiting scientists, students, and employees).

In addition, it can also be specified at this point what kind of research data the FD policy should cover, e.g. non-digital research data can be excluded or restrictions can be formulated, such as the restriction exclusively to research data from projects of one’s own institution.

The definition of range thus has an influence on the cost of RDM.

2.2 Relationship to legal requirements/contracts with third parties

The RDM must comply with all legal and internal regulations as well as Contracts with donors. A regulation on which requirements take precedence creates clarity for researchers.

2.2.1 What is the relationship to legal provisions and requirements of the institution?

The Research Data Management must comply with all legal and internal regulations as well as Contracts with donors. This means that the institution must ensure that the research data is collected, stored, and managed in accordance with relevant laws and regulations, as well as any agreements or contracts with donors or funding agencies. This may include requirements related to data privacy, data security, data sharing, and data preservation. The institution may also have its own internal policies and guidelines for research data management, which must be followed by researchers and other staff members. These policies may include requirements for data documentation, data organization, data access, and data reuse.

In addition, the institution may have specific requirements for research data management depending on the type of research being conducted and the funding sources involved. For example, some funding agencies may have specific requirements for data sharing and accessibility, or for the use of certain data management tools and platforms. Researchers must ensure that they are aware of and comply with these requirements in order to avoid any potential problems or delays in their research.

2.2.2 Which contracts have priority, especially in third-party funded research? and Do the contracts of third-party funding providers always take precedence or only if they contain stricter regulations, for example?

The priority of contracts in third-party-funded research is determined by the terms and conditions agreed upon by the parties involved. If the contracts of third-party funding providers contain stricter regulations than the institution’s internal regulations, then those regulations would take precedence. It is important to carefully review the terms and conditions of all contracts involved in order to determine which regulations have priority and ensure compliance with all legal and internal regulations.

2.2.3 What should be considered in the case of research collaborations?

In the case of research collaborations, it is important to ensure that all parties involved in the collaboration are aware of and agree to the terms and conditions for managing and sharing research data. This can include the types of data that will be collected and shared, who will have access to the data, and how the data will be stored and managed. It may also be necessary to consider any legal or ethical implications of the research, such as the need to obtain consent from individuals whose data is being used. Additionally, it may be necessary to clarify any intellectual property rights and ownership of the data. By carefully considering these issues and establishing clear agreements between all parties involved in the collaboration, researchers can ensure that the data is managed and shared in a responsible and transparent manner.

2.2.4 What is valid if projects have their own RD policy?

If projects have their own research data policies, those policies should be followed in regards to the management of research data. The specific requirements and regulations of the project’s research data policy should take precedence over any institutional or general policies. It is important to clearly define and communicate the research data policy for each project in order to ensure compliance and provide clarity for researchers.
