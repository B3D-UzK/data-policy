4  Responsibilities of Researchers

The individual areas of responsibility can be defined according to groups of persons (project management, all researchers, all members of the university, etc.) and differentiated according to the degree of commitment.

4.1 Researchers

4.1.1 Who is responsible for the RDM (project management, all researchers,etc.)?

The responsibility for research data management (RDM) should be clearly defined and communicated among all researchers involved in the project. In general, each researcher is responsible for managing the research data they generate, including ensuring its accuracy, completeness, and preservation. The project management team may also have additional responsibilities related to the overall management of research data within the project. It is important for all researchers to be aware of and adhere to the policies and standards established for RDM within the project.

4.1.2 How obligatory are the tasks?

The degree of obligation for tasks related to research data management (RDM) will depend on the specific policies and guidelines established by the research institution and the individual research project. In general, researchers are responsible for ensuring that their research data is managed correctly, completely, unadulterated, and reliably, and that the integrity of the research data is guaranteed. This may involve tasks such as properly documenting and organizing data, adhering to data sharing and licensing policies, and ensuring the long-term preservation of data. The exact responsibilities and obligations for researchers will vary depending on the specific circumstances of the research project and institution.

4.2 Principles RDM

4.2.1 According to which principles should FDM be operated?

  • Researchers are responsible for ensuring that their research data is managed correctly, completely, unaltered, and reliably, and that the integrity of the research data is guaranteed.

  • They should also be aware of and follow relevant international standards, such as the FAIR principles, which outline the key principles for responsible data management.

  • Researchers should ensure that they are familiar with the licensing rules that apply to their research data, and that they follow the appropriate rules for sharing and disseminating their data.

  • It is also important that researchers document the entire research cycle, including the tools and procedures used, and that they store their research data in a secure, long-term repository. This will ensure that the data is accessible and usable by other researchers in the future.

4.3 Data Management Plan (DMP)

4.3.1 Are researchers encouraged or encouraged to produce a DMP?

Researchers are encouraged to produce a Data Management Plan (DMP) to outline their plans for managing research data throughout the entire research cycle, including storage, documentation, and long-term preservation. A DMP can help researchers ensure that their research data is handled in accordance with established principles and standards, and can help support the responsible and sustainable management of research data

4.3.2 Should a DMP be created for all projects/new projects/for third-party funded projects/at what time?

Researchers are encouraged to produce a Data Management Plan (DMP) to outline their plans for managing research data throughout the entire research cycle, including storage, documentation, and long-term preservation. A DMP can help researchers ensure that their research data is handled in accordance with established principles and standards, and can help support the responsible and sustainable management of research data

4.3.3 Are the contents of a DMP executed (or defined in the appendix)?

4.3.4 Who creates the DMP?

Research projects with research data require (even before the start of the research project) a data management plan that sets out:

• a) where the research data will be stored,

• b) in what form the data will be stored,

• c) time and duration of storage and reasons for restrictions,

• d) how access will be designed.

4.4 Project Regulations

4.4.1 What regulations should the project managers make?

Project managers should ensure that their project adheres to any relevant laws and regulations, as well as any internal policies and guidelines that may be in place. This may include regulations related to data privacy, research ethics, financial management, and safety. Project managers should also be aware of any industry-specific regulations that may apply to their project. It is important for project managers to consult with relevant experts and stakeholders to ensure that they are aware of all applicable regulations and can create appropriate policies and procedures to ensure compliance

4.4.2 Should project managers define the conditions under which the research data will be made available?

4.4.3 Should regulations be made for the whereabouts of data in the event of a change of location?

4.4.4 Should plans be made for the period after the end of the project?

4.4.5 Should internal project guidelines be drawn up?

  • The project managers make arrangements for RDM in their projects, e.g. a determination at what time and under what conditions the research data will be made accessible.

  • The heads of research projects shape the RDM in their working groups and make arrangements when changing their members. These include the retention of the original data at the place of origin, precautions for the proper transfer of primary data and for the clarification of access rights, as well as the possibility of creating a duplicate of the data when changing jobs, provided that this does not conflict with data protection regulations.

  • The university recommends that those responsible for the project develop their own internal guidelines for RDM.

It is generally a good idea for project managers to create internal project guidelines to help ensure that research data is managed properly and in accordance with relevant laws and regulations. These guidelines should outline the policies and procedures that will be followed for data collection, storage, and access, and should be shared with all members of the project team. They should also be reviewed and updated regularly to ensure that they remain relevant and effective. In addition to helping ensure compliance with regulations, well-defined internal guidelines can also help improve the efficiency and productivity of the project by providing clear guidance for team members on how to handle data.

4.5 Defaults

4.5.1 What requirements do researchers have to comply with?

  • At RDM, researchers observe all organizational, legal, legal and contractual requirements.

  • All organizational, contractual, legal requirements must be complied with.

4.6 Training RDM

4.6.1 Are the project leaders required to inform students and doctoral candi-

dates about the handling of research data and to impart corresponding competences?

  • Students and doctoral candidates are to be informed about the adequate handling of research data and subject-specific competencies and standards are to be taught.

4.7 New Projects

New projects should be registered with [name of department] so that RDM can be ensured.
